Paleolithic Ketogenic Diet

The paleolithic ketogenic diet keeps
the body in constant ketosis. It uses
free fatty acids and ketone bodies like
beta-hydroxybutyrate, produced by the
liver from the free fatty acids for a
balanced supply of energy which is more
efficient than carbohydrates. One gram
of fat produces nine kilocalories,
while one gram of carbohydrates
produces only four kilocalories.

It must be noticed right in the beginning that ketosis
is not equivalent to ketoacidosis and neither does it
predispose to ketoacidosis. Contrary to popular belief,
the brain is able to use ketones to cover its energy
demand. The ketogenic mode is markedly better for the
brain than carbohydrate-based functioning. Use of ketones
bodies occurs in organells called mitochondria. Among the
cells in our body it is only red blood cells that cannot
use ketones for energy because they do not have
mitochondria. All other cells can use ketones for energy.
